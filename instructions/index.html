Overview

Glycogo aligns your food intake with your training demands.

Set personalised macronutrient targets, log meals, plan sessions, and monitor trends — all in one place. Follow the steps below to get up and running in minutes.

01

Log In

Create an account to save your data and enable syncing

Create an account to save food logs, custom foods, and settings, and to enable Intervals.icu sync. Your data is linked to your account and persists across sessions.

Food logs and custom foods saved to your account
Settings and targets remembered between visits
Required for Intervals.icu training sync
Trend history stored and available over time
02

Configure Settings

Set your bodyweight-based targets and training intensity scaling

Set your bodyweight and baseline macronutrient targets — carbohydrates, protein, and fat per kilogram of bodyweight. These form the foundation of your daily nutrition on rest days.

Then configure how your carbohydrate and calorie targets scale with session intensity. This is where Glycogo's periodisation logic lives — targets increase with RPE and duration, so you eat more on hard days and less on easy ones.

Start with the default scaling settings and adjust over time as you learn how your body responds to different fuelling levels.

See the Settings Reference below for a full description of every parameter and recommended starting ranges.

03

Build Your Food Database

Search USDA foods or add your own custom entries

Search thousands of foods from the USDA FoodData Central database, or add custom foods with full macro and serving-size control. Head to the Food Database tab to manage your library.

Search the USDA FoodData Central database for verified nutritional data
Add custom foods with your own macros and serving sizes
Edit macros and serving sizes at any time
Your custom foods are saved to your account for reuse
04

Add or Sync Training Sessions

Manual entry or automatic import via Intervals.icu

Enter session duration and RPE manually to calculate fuel targets, or sync planned and completed sessions via Intervals.icu — a free training hub that connects with Garmin, Wahoo, Polar, Suunto, Apple Watch, and more.

Each session generates its own pre-, during-, and post-session fuelling targets based on duration and RPE. These sit alongside your baseline daily targets so you always know how much to eat and when.

Intervals.icu is free to use and acts as a central hub that syncs data from most major fitness platforms. Connect it once and your sessions import automatically.
05

Nutrition Settings Reference

Full parameter descriptions and recommended starting ranges

Weight-Based Targets

These determine your baseline energy and macronutrient requirements without considering training expenditure.

MacronutrientRecommended Range
Carbohydratesg/kg2–4 g per kg bodyweight per day
Proteing/kg1.2–2 g per kg bodyweight per day
Fatg/kg1–1.5 g per kg bodyweight per day

Session-Based Parameters

These parameters control how your targets scale with training session intensity and duration.

ParameterDescription
Calories/hr Upper & Lower Range of extra calories per hour added above baseline during a session. Scales with RPE — at low RPE you hit the lower bound, at high RPE you approach the upper.
Carbohydrates/hr Upper & Lower Range of carbohydrates per hour consumed during training. Scales with RPE. The lower value applies to easy sessions; the upper to high-intensity efforts.
RPE Inflection Point The RPE at which carbohydrate intake rises most steeply. Lower values suit fuelling moderate sessions or gut-training. Higher values keep carbohydrate intake low until high-intensity efforts.
Steepness How sharply carbohydrates or calories ramp around the inflection point. Steep = rapid increase over a narrow RPE range. Shallow = gradual increase across multiple RPE levels.
Min Duration for Carbs Session must exceed this threshold (minutes) before within-session carbohydrates are added. Recommended: 60–90 min. Keeps carbohydrate targets appropriate for short sessions.
